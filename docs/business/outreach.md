# Outreach scripts

*Principle (Jack): show, don't pitch. Every message leads with something we already made for* them, *not a service menu. Volume is not the strategy — ten researched messages beat two hundred blasts. Voice: plain, terse, no exclamation marks.*

## Before any message

For each prospect, spend 10 minutes:
1. Load their current site on a phone. Note the two worst things (speed, dead links, no menu, no age gate — screenshot them).
2. Search "[their category] near me" from their neighborhood. Note who outranks them.
3. Optional power move: have Claude build a one-screen redesign mock of *their* homepage in the Ahtomic system. Attach it. This costs you 20 minutes and closes more than anything else in this file.

## Email 1 — the specific observation (cold)

**Subject:** [Their business] — two things on your site

> Hi [NAME],
>
> I searched "[category] near [their city]" this week and [COMPETITOR] shows up before you. Two reasons why, from looking at your site: [SPECIFIC THING 1] and [SPECIFIC THING 2].
>
> I run Ahtomic Studio — small web studio in [CITY]. We build fast, compliant sites that businesses can edit themselves. [ONE-LINE PROOF: "We also built CannaPickForMe — you may know it."]
>
> If it's useful, I'll send over a free mock of what your homepage could look like. No strings — worst case you got a free second opinion.
>
> Adam
> ahtomic.com

*Why it works: the observation proves effort, the mock offer is show-don't-pitch, and "no strings" is true.*

## Email 2 — follow-up with the mock (3–5 days later, send regardless of reply)

**Subject:** that mock — [their business]

> Hi [NAME] — went ahead and made the mock: [LINK or attached image].
>
> That's your actual menu and branding, loading in under a second. Takes us about two weeks to ship the real thing, fixed price, and you can edit it yourself after.
>
> Worth a 15-minute call? [TWO TIME OPTIONS]. If not, keep the mock.

## Walk-in / DM variant (dispensaries)

> "Hey — I built CannaPickForMe, the strain-matcher app. I was checking out dispensary sites in [AREA] and made a quick redesign of yours for fun. Want me to send it? I run a web studio; if you like it, we can talk. If not, it's yours."

## The no-list

- No "I hope this email finds you well."
- No feature lists in first contact. Outcome and proof only.
- No "just following up" — every touch adds something new (the mock, a Lighthouse score, a ranking screenshot).
- Stop after three touches. Add them to a quarterly "still here, here's what we shipped lately" list instead.

## After the first yes

Optimize delivery for the case study: get before/after numbers (speed, ranking position, signups in month one) and a two-sentence quote. That case study *is* the outreach for prospect #2 — the ladder is review → replicate.
