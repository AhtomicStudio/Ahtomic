# Ahtomic Studio — positioning one-pager

*Framework: Jack Roberts' Avatar Problem Method. Honest version — no headline-number fantasies. Revise after every client conversation; this document is a hypothesis until someone pays.*

## The avatar (pick one, stay on it)

**Recommendation: cannabis dispensaries and cannabis-adjacent brands in California.**

Why this wedge and not "any small business":
- You already own a live product in their world (CannaPickForMe) — instant credibility no generalist agency has.
- You understand their compliance constraints (age gates, labeling, ad-platform bans) — a real moat, since most web shops don't.
- Dispensaries already pay for digital presence (menus, Weedmaps/Leafly listings, SEO agencies) — proof of budget.
- They're reachable: you can walk in, and CannaPickForMe gives you a reason to be talking to them anyway.

Second avatar (later, don't split focus now): CA service businesses referred from the first wedge.

## The painful, expensive, recurring problem

A dispensary's web presence is usually a menu embed on a template site. It doesn't rank, doesn't convert first-time visitors, and can't be updated without calling someone. They lose walk-ins to whichever competitor shows up first in "dispensary near me," and they pay listing platforms for customers they never own.

**What we remove:** the dependency. A fast site they own, that ranks locally, converts visitors into loyalty-program signups they keep, and that they can edit themselves.

## The offer (outcome, not deliverables)

> "A website that turns searches into first visits — live in two weeks, compliant, and yours to edit."

- Built and shipped in days, not agency-months. One person directs; AI agents build. No handoffs.
- Includes the boring-but-critical parts: local SEO, page speed, age-gate compliance, analytics.
- A CMS they can actually use (we run one ourselves — this site).
- Optional monthly care: updates, content changes, monitoring. This is the retainer that compounds.

## Pricing posture

Price the outcome, not hours. Anchor against what they already pay (platform fees, an SEO agency retainer, a lost customer's lifetime value).

- **Build:** fixed price per blueprint. Start where you're credible; raise after each case study.
- **Care retainer:** monthly, scoped to response time + a change budget. Recurring revenue is the business; the build is the door.
- First 1–2 clients: optimize for a *referenceable result*, not revenue. Discount in exchange for a named case study and a review — say that out loud in the proposal.

## Proof strategy (the Review → replicate ladder)

1. Ship one dispensary site that measurably beats the old one (speed, ranking, signups). Free or cheap is fine.
2. Turn it into a one-page case study with numbers.
3. Same blueprint, next dispensary. The pitch becomes "here's what happened for X."
